<p><p>A refracting telescope, also known as a dioptric telescope, is a type of optical telescope that uses a lens to focus light and form an image. It is the oldest type of telescope and operates on the principle of refraction. The refracting telescope design consists of an objective lens at the front that gathers light and focuses it to form an image. The image is then magnified using an eyepiece lens at the back of the telescope. Refracting telescopes offer high-resolution images and are well-suited for observing celestial objects, terrestrial landscapes, and wildlife.</p><p>The refracting telescope market is witnessing steady growth due to several factors.
