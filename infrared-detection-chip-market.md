<p><p>Infrared detection chips are used in various applications for detecting heat patterns and producing corresponding electrical signals. The market types for these chips can be classified into two categories - uncooled type and cooled type. </p><p>Uncooled type infrared detection chips do not require external cooling mechanisms. They are designed with microbolometers that can detect infrared radiation and convert it into electrical signals. These chips are cost-effective, compact, and widely used in consumer electronics, automotive, and security systems.</p><p>Cooled type infrared detection chips, on the other hand, use a cooling mechanism to achieve higher sensitivity and accuracy. They are equipped with cryogenic cooling systems such as Stirling or Joule-Thomson coolers. These chips are expensive and commonly used in military, scientific, and industrial applications that demand superior performance and fine-tuned measurements.</p></p>
